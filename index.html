Spring Break Abroad

CORE is taking you abroad this spring break! From March 13th to March 19th, our Global Tech Treks program gives you the option of traveling to Berlin, London, Paris, or Rio de Janeiro to explore these cities' tech scenes. You'll be meeting with local entrepreneurs and venture capitalists, touring startup offices, and, of course, exploring the city's popular tourist attractions.

Locations and Company Highlights

London

Explore the vibrant, diverse city of London! Learn about the Silicon Alley of Europe before going to popular destinations such as the London Eye, the British Gallery, and Parliament.

Palantir Technologies aims to solve the most important problems for the world's most important institutions. Since 2004, Palantir has doubled in size every year while retaining startup values.
One of Europe's largest early-stage venture capital firms, Balderton has a track record of helping entrepreneurs build substantial businesses.
Having raised over $90M in funding, TransferWise is a money transfer service that allows individuals and businesses to send money abroad without hidden charges.
Cities are complicated. Citymapper uses the power of mobile and open transport data to help humans survive and master them.

Paris

Join students from Ecole Polytechnique and the University of Tokyo to visit both budding startups and established accelerators, all while enjoying the beautiful sights Paris provides!

NUMA accelerates the actors of innovation: startups, communities, and organizations in Paris and all over the world. The organization is on a mission to show that French innovation is alive and well.
Iris Capital is a venture capital firm specializing in the digital economy. It values experienced teams, cutting edge technologies, high-growth markets, and business models that are viable and sustainable.
Founded in 2002, Elaia Partners is a venture capital fund managing over €135 million. Elaia Partners aims to lead the initial financing round and plays a hands-on role in supporting entrepreneurs.
Agoranov incubator fosters the creation of innovative startups by providing a professional and friendly environment anlong with access to expertise, tools and infrastructure crucial to success and rapid growth.

Berlin

Berlin's exciting culture has resulted in what may be Europe's fastest growing startup scene! Hear success stories from Berlin's best, then wander through the city's many historic landmarks.

An audio platform that attracts more than 175 million unique monthly listeners. Soundcloud enables sound creators to upload, promote, record, and share their music.
A simple task management tool and one of the most popular to-do list apps that exist today. Wunderlist has over 13 million users and was acquired by Microsoft earlier in 2015.
Blinklist is committed to helping you read more in less time. The platform condenses over 1,000 best-selling non-fiction books into powerful packs that you can read in just 15 minutes.
Founded in Berlin, Delivery Hero is the world’s largest network of online food ordering sites which operates in 21 countries and with over 73,000 restaurant partners.

Rio de Janeiro

Delve into some of South America's most exciting startups. Afterwards, take the time to visit grand sports arenas, elaborate cathedrals, and sunny beaches.

PeixeUrbano enables users to find and book deals on restaurants, entertainment activities, trade services, and more based on their locations and preferences.
ChefsClub is transforming the dining industry by utilizing technology and a subscription-based service to fill empty tables at great restaurants without neglecting the customer experience.
Passei Direto is a social network with a mission of connecting students and their knowledge. It assists students in finding study materials, meeting colleagues, and searching for internships and jobs.
Sieve provides retailers with intelligent price and inventory management so they can optimize their business. Notable clients include Microsoft, Sony, and Johnson & Johnson.

Student Testimonials

"The CORE Spring Break tech trek was the most exciting and worthy event I was a part of this year, in no small part due to the excellent programming of the trip. Out of the companies we go to see, my favorite was Rentify. The people that we met in their office were so funny and intelligent, and I felt extremely lucky that we had gotten to meet them one-on-one. I learned a lot of lessons about the European tech scene and its growth trajectory, as well as a ton about European culture (take-out becomes take-away!), since I'd never been to the continent before. My favorite moment on the trip was probably staring out at the Thames and Big Ben from the top of the London Eye, which was a breathtaking view. I can't wait to go on other tech treks next year and learn more about other parts of the world and their tech scenes." —Mayank Mahajan, SEAS '17
"Ask a bunch of college students, 'Want to spend your spring break in Paris immersing yourself in the tech and entrepreneurship scene of the city?' and you’ve got yourself quite the self-selected bunch. While I was expecting traipsing around the city with intermittent visits to startup offices, instead I had the privilege of attending intimate presentations from successful founders of hardware and fintech startups, experiencing an exhilarating and bilingual pitch night alongside business students from the city, and gorging on croissants while learning about France’s unique startup funding space from venture capitalists. On our visit to one of France’s most prominent tech accelerators, a founder shared some poignant insight: 'Be an entrepreneur because you can’t imagine yourself being anything else.' The tenacity, innovation, and hustle that I had the opportunity to witness during my short week in Paris taught me that this sentiment is universal, and entrepreneurship is a powerful global phenomenon that can solve technical and social problems against all odds. Now, a spring break without remixed renditions of Roar-Lion-Roar and Aux Champs Elyseés is a break I’d rather go without!” —Supriya Jain, SEAS '16

Frequently Asked Questions

When will the trips be?

The trips will depart on Sunday, March 13th and arrive back at Columbia on Saturday, March 19th.

What will accomodations be?

Students going on the Paris trip will be hosted by Ecole Polytechnique students. All other trips will have prearranged hotel lodging.

Can GS/Barnard students apply?

Yes! However, please check for possible visa issues you may encounter traveling to your destination of choice.

How many people can go on each trip?

Spots are limited to 15 people for each trip, so apply now!

Do I need to be invested in entrepreneurship to apply?

No! If you show that you're excited to learn about the global entrepreneurship scene, we'll be happy to consider you!

Will there be time for tourist activities?

Of course! Most of our company visits end around 5pm, leaving plenty of time to explore.

Can international students apply?

If you're a non-US citizen and require a Visa to travel abroad, we ask that you apply to our Paris program—where our partners at École Polytechnique will provide the necessary documentation—or to our Rio program.

How much will the trips cost?

Thanks to generous sponsors and our partner Columbia Entrepreneurship, costs will be limited to $850 per person which will cover round-trip flight and housing. Meals and local transportation are not covered by the $850. However, Brazil will be an additional $160 for US citizens due to visa costs.

Will financial aid be available?

Unfortunately, we are already providing substantial amounts of financial aid to subsidize the cost for everyone, so we are unable to provide financial aid on an individual basis.
